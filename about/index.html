Kohei Masuda

Data journalist and interactive developer. I build stories about Japan — told through maps, charts, and scrollytelling visualizations.

I'm a developer with a background in iOS and data visualization, currently focused on data journalism. My work lives at the intersection of statistics and storytelling — taking public datasets and finding the human questions hiding inside them.

Most of my projects explore Japan: its demographics, geography, culture, and the economic forces reshaping its cities. I work primarily with D3.js for interactive graphics, and I care a lot about getting the data right before I care about making it look good.

Previous work includes MementoMori, an iOS app built with SwiftUI and SwiftData, and a series of standalone interactive maps covering Japan's Starbucks locations, James Turrell installations, and Hiroshi Sugimoto artworks.
